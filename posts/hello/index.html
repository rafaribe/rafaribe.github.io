Hello World

Hello, world! Isn’t this the first program we all write when trying out a new programming language? I guess this makes it a cool title for this blog post as well. This is my first post on my blog and as such I would like to introduce myself.

I’m Rafael Ribeiro, I’m 28 years old and I like to work with computers. I was born and raised in a small village in the Portuguese interior but since early I liked to play around with computers. At first to play games and at about 15 years of age, I found myself choosing a course for my 10th-year Secondary School which vaguely translates to the “Informatic Technological Course”.

In there I had the first contact with programming, my first editor was Turbo Pascal? Remember that? It looked like this:

Yeah, I know right? That’s grandpa’s code editor!

Anyway, in college, I got contact with even more programming languages such as C++, C#, Visual Basic, Prolog, Lisp, to name a few, and also touched in other areas that I find interesting, such as networking and systems administration. I got into programming and started working for a company after finishing up my graduation and while taking a Master’s Degree.

I worked for three years in a single project, mainly doing C#, SQL Server, and being your Jack-of-all-trades, do everything kind of guy.

I’ve taken a new job since the beginning of the year and, by my choice, decided to embrace that inner sysadmin in me and leaned more towards a DevOps role in the company, dealing with cloud infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines and also programming, I didn’t stop programming. It is an exciting thing for me. I’ve started learning and utilizing some exciting technologies such as:

  • Docker
  • Kubernetes
  • Flux
  • Loki
  • Prometheus
  • Traefik
  • cdk8s
  • Java with Spring
  • Pulumi for Infrastructure provisioning
  • and many more…

This also made me in love with cloud-native software.

One of the other things that I’ve changed is my engagement with the community.

I’ve attended some meetups and webinars like crazy the past few months, and they have proven to me to be such a valuable tool to learn new things, that I can’t imagine how I went all these past years without them.

It’s an exciting time, and launching this new blog I hope to share a bit of my experience with the community, do some tutorials and maybe help someone in some way.
